Use the Keyboard
Before you log on to ICTV services, you should know how to use the Keyboard. If you're an experienced computer user and familiar with a trackball and cursors, skip this part and continue on.

If you're not a computer user or unfamiliar with a trackball, look at the photograph of the Keyboard and review its functions as described on page 5. You may also want to try out your keyboard at this time. You won't be able to see anything on your television screen (unless you're connected to ICTV services), but you'll be able to get a feel for the keys and trackball. Take note, getting used to the trackball and cursor buttons can be tricky—don't get frustrated and remember the old adage, "practice makes perfect."

The Keyboard
Keyboard Function Description
Cursor Buttons Two cursor buttons are located at the top left of the Keyboard. As you move the trackball, the cursor or pointer on your television screen moves. The cursor shows your location on the screen or may be used as an insertion point. Depending on it's location on the screen, the cursor can change its appearance to a pointer shaped as an arrow, an I-beam, or a hand with a pointing index finger.

The arrow indicates your location on the screen.

The I-beam looks like a flashing I and is used for entering and editing text.

The hand with a pointing index finger appears as you pass over a linked area, graphics or pictures. This means that related Internet sites are connected or linked to the item on the screen.
Left Cursor Button A single click (a quick press and release of the button) lets you select an item. For example, to bring another page of information to your screen you would position the pointer to the link you want to select, then you would quickly press down the left cursor button.
Right Cursor Button When pressed, displays a selection menu on the screen.
Trackball The round ball at the top right-hand side of the Keyboard controls the location of the cursor. As you roll the trackball, the cursor moves accordingly on the screen.
Arrow Keys Located on the lower right-hand side of the Keyboard. As you press an arrow key the screen scrolls in the direction the arrow is pointing.
Pg Up Scrolls the page up one screen at a time. If scroll bars appear on your screen, you will notice the scroll bars move up when you press the Pg Up key.
Pg Dn Scrolls the page down one screen at a time. If scroll bars appear on your screen, you will notice the scroll bars move down when you press the Pg Dn key.
Keys Act like a typewriter to display characters (letters, numbers and symbols) at appropriate locations on the screen.
Click-on, Clicking on
Click-on refers to using the cursor to select an item on the screen, such as a browser button or link. To click-on an object, move the trackball so the cursor is pointed on the text or graphic you want to select, then quickly press and release the left cursor button.

Use the Scroll Bars
Sometimes there is more information available than will fit on the screen. In these cases scroll bars appear (there should be a scroll bar on the right side of the TV screen right now. You can use the arrow keys to scroll more information onto the screen.

Sometimes there are several areas on screen that can be scrolled. The arrow keys scroll the active region. You can make a region active by clicking in it.

The ICTV Services Home Page
The Web is an electronic collection of different services and resources available over the Internet. This information is organized onto pages that you can call to your television screen using the browser. The browser is a software application used to view information on the Web. It helps you move from one Web site to another by clicking on highlighted text and pictures (links and buttons).

Once you've logged on to ICTV services, the ICTV Services Home Page is the first page you'll see displayed. This is the page the Web browser automatically starts at and where you'll begin to explore the Web.

Each item or topic listed on the ICTV Services Home Page is a link. When you click-on the link, information about that topic will be displayed on your screen. For example, the GAMES link provides you with a selection of games to play. Before you click-on a link, read about Navigation Buttons (located below) as the information will help you get around easier.

Clicking on the spinning globe in the upper left will bring you back to the ICTV Services Home Page.

Browser Navigation Buttons
Browser Navigation Buttons are buttons located on the toolbar at the top of your television screen.

These buttons appear on every page and allow you to perform specific tasks on that page or help you to quickly move to another page. To select or click-on a button, use the trackball to move the arrow cursor to position it on the desired navigation button and then press the left cursor button. Each of the navigation buttons are described on the next page.

Back Displays the previous page viewed. The browser maintains a list of pages (history list) you visited during your current session.
Forward Moves you one screen ahead to the next page in the history list.
Reload Click the RELOAD button if the image on your television appears distorted or incomplete. This feature will refresh the displayed page.
Zoom Displays a pop-up window which turns the pointer into a magnifier. See More on the ZOOM Button provided on this page.
Add Site Allows you to save and store Web addresses or locations. When you click-on this button, you'll see a message informing you that your Bookmark has been added. The Bookmark is added to the Bookmark section of the GOTO SITE. This provides quick access the next time you want to visit the site. To exit this page, press the BACK button and the page you were previously on will be displayed. See More on Bookmarks on the next page.
Goto Site Displays a box where you type in addresses for a specific Web site. Press the ENTER key and the site will display on your screen. Your Bookmark list is also located on the GOTO SITE page. Click-on a location on your list and you'll go there. See More on the GOTO SITE Button on the next page.
Home ICTV icon located in the upper right hand corner of the screen. When clicked, you'll return to the ICTV Services Home Page. The ICTV icon rotates when you click-on a link. This is a visible indication that the computer (at the Headend) is in the retrieval process.

More on the Zoom Button
The ZOOM button allows you to enlarge objects or text on the screen. To activate the Zoom feature, move the trackball to position the pointer on the Zoom Button. Click-on the Zoom Button and a pop-up window displays on your screen. You can move the zoom window around by "dragging" it to another location on the screen. Move the cursor to the title bar of the ZOOM box (top edge). Click-on the left mouse button while moving the track ball. This will "drag" the box to a different location on the screen.

To increase the magnification, position the cursor in the ZOOM pop-up window and press the left cursor button. You'll see the 2X change to 3X and so on. To decrease the magnification, press the right cursor button.

To stop the ZOOM feature, click-on the X in the top corner of the pop-up window. If a Back Forward pop-up window appears when you press the right cursor button, press the B key to move back or the F key to move forward, or press the Esc key to get rid of the box.

More on Bookmarks
A bookmark is a saved Web address that you can go to instantly. Bookmarks are also known as "favorites" (favorite places) or "hotlist" (hot entry).You should consider making bookmarks when you want to frequently access a Web page quickly. For example, to read ESPN news or review the latest stock prices. Or, if you get to a page you find interesting, but don't have the time to explore it and all its links, then make a bookmark for that page.

Remember, select the ADD SITE button to add a bookmark. You can have as many bookmarks as you want and they are easy to get rid of.

More on the GOTO SITE Button
A list of your Bookmarks are displayed when you select the GOTO SITE button. Bookmarks you added are denoted with an X to the left of the Bookmark item.

To delete a bookmark, click-on the X of the bookmark item you want to remove. A pop-up window will display asking you to confirm your delete request. Click-on OK to delete or Cancel to cancel the delete. When you click-on OK, a Delete Bookmark screen will display confirming that the bookmark was deleted. To exit the Delete Bookmark page, select one of the navigation buttons.

Advertisements
By now you've probably noticed the advertisements on the ICTV Services Home Page. Advertisements display for approximately five (5) seconds before the next one appears. If you want to know more about the product or service, click on the advertisement and you'll automatically go to the site that provides additional information.

Frequently Asked Questions
Movies don't play.

The ICTV system is very fast (up to 10Mbps). Even at this speed, some movies require 10 to 30 seconds to download before they are ready to play. Also some of the sites which have the movies can be overloaded during the day causing slower than normal access times. Many times patience will pay off (the approximate time remaining to download the movie is displayed in the lower left of the screen). Remember once a movie loads you need to click on the movie image to play the movie or click the right mouse button to display a menu of movie viewing options.

Sound clips don't play.

The ICTV system is very fast (up to 10Mbps). Even at this speed, some sound clips require 10 to 30 seconds to download before they are ready to play. Also some of the sites which have the sound clips can be overloaded during the day causing slower than normal access times. Many times patience will pay off. (Unfortunately, at this time the sound player does not provide the time remaining to complete the download

Attempting to access some sound files and movie files results in Missing plugin error.

ICTV has initially configured the system to support some of the more popular video and audio content that ends with .AVI or .WAV. ICTV is currently qualifying software which will support Quick Time and RealAudio content (these files end with .MOV and .RA). ICTV will make this additional software available as soon as it is thoroughly tested in our laboratories.

Attempting to send e-mail messages results in E-mail Address not configured message.

ICTV has initially configured the system without support for e-mail. E-mail support is currently under development and may be made available in the future.

Attempting to download a file displays an error message.

The ICTV system does not currently support the ability to download and permanently store files. This includes files that end with .ZIP. Click the cancel button to clear the error message
